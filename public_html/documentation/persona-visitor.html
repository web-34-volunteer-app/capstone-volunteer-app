Persona: New User/Visitor

Name:

Kendra Foxx

Profession:

Kendra is a part-time undergrad student working toward a degree in social science. She lives at home while attending school and has extra time on her hand due to not having to work and only attending school part-time. She feels like volunteering could help get her some useful experience and learn more about social interactions to provide significant insights into her studies.

Technology:

Kendra is on a budget and only has a consumer-level PC laptop for her studies. She has an iPhone to communicate with her friends who moved away and check social media. She is more of a moderate technology user and is very practical in her technology use.

Attitudes and Behaviors:

Kendra wants her journey in self-growth and education to be broad to have more to offer the world. She is open to doing as much as possible if she is learning and able to help others.

Frustration:

Kendra wants to volunteer but is limited in her ability to travel due to not having a car and relying on public transportation. She may need a ride from time to time to get to volunteer locations.

Goal:

Kendra finds a volunteer opportunity valuable to her education and personal goals. There is plenty of opportunity within walking distance of her home; for those opportunities not close enough to walk, there’s also a possibility for people offering ride-share.

User Story:

As a potential volunteer, I discover an opportunity that is perfect for me. All obstacles are removed for me to participate; I decide to register to volunteer and get more information.

Case & Interaction Flows

Case #1

Kendra is searching for an easy-to-navigate site that meets all of her volunteer limiting concerns and lands on the MissionCitizen site; she finds the site intuitive and addresses her participation concerns, so she registers for an account.

  • Pre-condition: Kendra is on MissionCitizen’s landing page and now pursuing the page searching for volunteer opportunities.
  • Post-condition: Kendra finds the site intuitive to use and wants to participate in the site’s initiative; Kendra registers for an account.

Interaction Flow: Create an account

  • Kendra is automatically impressed and clicks on the register for an account link/button on the top right.
  • A form pops up, and she fills out her details and clicks submit.
  • Her account is automatically approved, and she is now in a registered user view, where she can now personalize her profile, register for events, and send messages to event organizers.

Case #2

Kendra lands on the site’s homepage and is interested in what the site has to offer.

  • Pre-condition: Kendra is on the landing page.
  • Post-condition: Kendra is impressed with site's mission and ability to encourage community volunteerism.

Interaction Flow: Interacts with carousel of images and testimonials

  • Kendra is intrigued by the photos and testimonial messages at the top of the page.
  • The photo carousel frame alternates between photo and testimonial messages.
  • Kendra clicks on the arrow located on either side of the photo/message frame.
  • The photo/message returns to the previous photo/message.
  • Kendra moves on and leaves it alone.
  • The photo/message frame reverts to its default animation.

Case #3

Kendra lands on the site’s homepage and is exploring what the site has to offer.

  • Pre-condition: Kendra is on the landing page.
  • Post-condition: Kendra signs up for an account after finding a great opportunity using the search field and map feature.

Interaction Flow: Uses search box and map feature

Kendra explores opportunities and signs up for an account.

  • Kendra types in the search box with keywords indicating a location and her interests.
  • On-screen Map populates with a zoomed-in view of a location with clickable pins marking volunteer events matching her keywords.
  • Kendra clicks a pin.
  • A dialogue box opens describing the event details, including whether it offers a transportation option; it also has a link for her to register for an account and get more info or apply for the event.
  • Kendra clicks on the register for an account link.
  • A form pops up, and she fills out her details and clicks submit.
  • Her account is automatically approved, and she is now in a registered user view, where she can now personalize her profile, register for events, and send messages to event organizers.
