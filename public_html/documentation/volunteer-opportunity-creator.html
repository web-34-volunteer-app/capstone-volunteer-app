Persona: Volunteer Opportunity Creator

Name:

Christina Jimenez

Profession:

Volunteer manager at Paving Paths, a nonprofit focused on mentorship and bridging gaps in education for at risk youth. She also serves on the board of Nonprofits New Mexico. Christina’s organization relies on volunteers to provide various services. Due to COVID restrictions, they were unable to have in-person volunteers. However, as restrictions are being lifted, Christina is tasked with promoting and recruiting volunteers. She is looking for a website where she can share upcoming volunteer opportunities with her community.

Technology:

13.3” MacBook Pro (macOS Catalina, version 10.15.7 operating system), iPhone X 4G speed unlimited data (iOS 14.1 operating system). As a practiced user of technology, Christina possesses intermediate computer skills. She is comfortable installing programs, navigating websites, and completing daily tasks such as viewing emails, using her calendar and checking social media websites.

Attitudes and Behaviors:

Christina is an extrovert and loves attending community events. She is an avid net-worker and thrives in finding opportunities to create new relationships. She believes everyone can make a difference. She prefers getting to know people personally rather than just as a business opportunity. She wants to make the most out of her connections to grow her organization and raise awareness for topics that matter to her the most. Christina favors websites that effectively meet her needs and are visually informative. She enjoys recruiting, training and managing volunteers.

Frustration:

Christina is frustrated by rude and thoughtless individuals. She is often in a crunch for time as she manages several positions. She dislikes excessive reliance on social media and poor communication. Christina is looking for passionate, dependable, competent and trustworthy individuals to volunteer at her organization.

Goal:

To share volunteer opportunities with her community and leverage their skills to foster education and mentorship amongst youth.

User Story:

As a user, I need to have a platform where I can share opportunities to increase volunteers who can help fulfill our mission.

Case & Interaction Flows

Case #1

Christina will be using web platforms to increase volunteer outreach and share upcoming opportunities.

  • Pre-condition: Christina searched for a volunteer website and selected Mission Citizen.
  • Post-condition: Christina has created a Mission Citizen user profile.

Interaction Flow: Create an account

  • Christina clicks on the “Sign Up” option listed on the homepage.
  • Mission Citizen displays a form requesting information needed to create an account.
  • Christina enters her name, email, phone number and creates a username and password.
  • Christina then provides a reCAPTCHA verification and selects that she agrees to the community guidelines before clicking submit.
  • Mission Citizen creates a profile, stores Christina’s information and finally, displays Christina’s account page.

Case #2

Christina has created a Mission Citizen profile and wants to begin creating and sharing opportunities.

  • Pre-condition: Christina is logged into her Mission Citizen profile.
  • Post-condition: Christina has created an upcoming volunteer opportunity for Paving Paths.

Interaction Flow: Create volunteer opportunities

  • Christina visits the Missions Citizen website and selects the login option.
  • Mission Citizen displays a page requesting the user’s username and password.
  • Christina provides her username and password and clicks login.
  • Mission Citizen displays her user profile page.
  • Christina selects the option to create a new event.
  • Mission Citizen displays a new event form.
  • Christina enters the organization’s name, event details such as volunteer requirements, date, time, name and description of the event.
  • Christina confirms details and selects the option to post it.
  • Mission Citizen adds event to list of opportunities on its data base and displays it on the opportunity map and Christina’s user profile.
  • Christina selects log out option.
  • Mission Citizen logs out Christina and returns to the home page.

Case #3

Christina’s Day in The Zoo event is coming up and she would like to send the volunteers an email with details regarding the event parking and provide her contact information in case her assistance is needed.

  • Pre-condition: Christina has event information she needs to share with volunteers.
  • Post-condition: Christina has sent an email providing additional details.

Interaction Flow: Contact volunteers

Christina visits the Missions Citizen website and selects the login option.

  • Mission Citizen displays a page requesting the user’s username and password.
  • Christina provides her username and password and clicks log in.
  • Mission Citizen displays her user profile page.
  • Christina reviews upcoming events displayed on her dashboard.
  • Christina selects the email icon next to one of the upcoming events.
  • Mission Citizen displays an email form allowing her to send a mass email to all volunteers registered.
  • Christina enters details about parking and provides her contact information.
  • Christina clicks send.
  • Mission Citizen sends a mass email to all registered volunteers and displays a copy of the email in Christina’s inbox.
  • Christina selects log out option.
  • Mission Citizen logs out Christina and returns to the home page.

Case #4

Christina has been notified that the Day in The Zoo’s time has been changed from 4pm-6pm MST to 3:00pm-5:00pm MST. She will need to update the event and notify volunteers.

  • Pre-condition: Christina’s event details have changed.
  • Post-condition: Christina has updated event details and notified volunteers.

Interaction Flow: Edit and delete event information

  • Christina visits the Missions Citizen website and selects the login option.
  • Mission Citizen displays a page requesting the user’s username and password.
  • Christina provides her username and password and clicks log in.
  • Mission Citizen displays her user profile page.
  • Christina moves cursor to the events section on her user profile and selects the desired upcoming event.
  • Mission Citizen provides an overview of the event and displays the option to edit or delete the event as identified by a X icon and a pencil icon respectively.
  • Christina selects the edit option and updates the time of the event.
  • Mission Citizen updates the changes on their data base and sends email notifying registered volunteers.
  • Christina selects log out option.
  • Mission Citizen logs out Christina and returns to the home page.

Case #5

Christina’s Day at The Zoo has been completed. She needs to calculate volunteer hours for her program’s report.

  • Pre-condition: Christina’s event has ended.
  • Post-condition: Christina has logged the volunteer hours on her Mission Citizen profile.

Interaction Flow: Track and approve

  • Christina visits the Missions Citizen website and selects the login option.
  • Mission Citizen displays a page requesting the user’s username and password.
  • Christina provides her username and password and clicks log in.
  • Mission Citizen displays her user profile page.
  • Christina navigates to the desired event and selects the event hour tracker option as identified by a stopwatch icon.
  • Mission Citizen displays a calculating form.
  • Christina enters information regarding volunteer event and hours completed.
  • Mission Citizens adds hours and displays them on her profile page.
  • Christina views hours on her profile and proceeds to log out.
  • Mission Citizen logs out Christina and returns to the home page.
