Persona: Volunteer/Seeker

Name:

John Filer

Profession:

John is a first-class petty officer in the navy. It is strongly encouraged, in order to be competitive for the next rank, to perform extra-curricular activities and participate in the community.

Technology:

John is an average PC user with a smart phone. He uses his computer mostly for administrative functions, such as e-mail or news, and leverages his devices for connecting with friends and family.

Attitudes and Behaviors:

John is outwardly friendly, and ambitious. He has a singular focus and, in an attempt to be successful in his career, accumulates as many collateral duties at work, volunteer hours, and educational credits as possible.

Frustration:

John has some extra time during the week to volunteer, but is finding it difficult to find great volunteer opportunities that fold nicely into his schedule.

Goal:

John wants a one-stop location to find all the volunteer opportunities he can so that he can engineer his schedule for success.

User Story:

As an average user, John wants to stay up to date with volunteer events in the community and pack in as much volunteering as possible into his full schedule.

Case #1

Visit the website and stay up to date with saved volunteer events.

  • Pre-condition: User is a registered user using a device that was previously logged into.
  • Post-condition: John has checked his upcoming events.

Interaction Flow:

  • User: Navigates to the website home page on his smartphone.
  • System: Since he regularly visits the website from this device, the system maintains his login. His username is displayed in the navigation bar to indicate he is still logged in. The welcome banner is displayed below the navigation bar; just below that is a list of hyperlinks describing upcoming dates of volunteer events, event names, and contact lists for the event.
  • User: He clicks on the top event name, which is the soonest to occur.
  • System: A new page appears (either replacing whole page or a frame of the home page), displaying the event name, date, point of contact (POC), details, address, and a list of other available contacts. There is a checkbox to control privacy and allow other volunteers involved with this event to contact John. There is a green light indication in the top left indicating that John is registered to attend this event. There are buttons at the bottom of the form to unregister from the event, contact the POC, or flag suspicious activity.

Case #2

Search for new volunteer events and register for an event.

  • Pre-condition: John has logged in and checked his upcoming events.
  • Post-condition: John has searched for new events and registered for a new event.

Interaction Flow:

  • User: He clicks the "Return (or Home)" link.
  • System: The home page reappears.
  • User: He types his zip code into the search filter that appears just below his event list. He enters a date range from now to four weeks from now and clicks the search button.
  • System: The interactive map displayed below the search filter zooms to enlarge his zip code region and is populated with pins of different colors, indicating different classifications of events. Mousing over pins will display the event name, date, and a brief description. Below (Or perhaps to the left, like google map directions) the map is a table of all the events queried, along with dates, event names, address, point of contact, etc.
  • User: He clicks the name of an event that falls on a date he needs to fill.
  • System: The event page is again displayed. The green light indication is red, indicating that John is not registered for this event. The "Unregister" button instead says "Register".
  • User: He clicks the "Register" button.
  • System: A pop-up window indicates "You are registered for this event." The "Register" button reverts to "Unregister" and the red light/text indicator becomes green, indicating he is registered for the event.

Case #4:

View user profile.

  • Pre-condition: User in on the home page.
  • Post-condition: John views his user profile.

Interaction Flow:

  • User: He clicks his user name/user icon in the top right/left corner.
  • System: A form appears displaying pertinent information: user name, real name, date of account, volunteer hours and events history, privacy preferences (can other volunteers or organizers contact you). There is a link to download a detailed .csv file for volunteer history details.
